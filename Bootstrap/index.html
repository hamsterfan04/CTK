Illinois

The Prairie State

Illinois was declared a state on December 3rd, 1818, and was the 21st state to become part of the Union. It is part of the midwestern US, a region comprised of 12 states. Those 12 states are, as stated by the US Census Bureau, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, North Dakota, Ohio, South Dakota, and Wisconsin.
Illinois’ capital city is the city of Springfield, which is located in central Illinois. The most well known city in the state is Chicago, which is located in the northeast. As of 2024, Illinois has a population of 12.7 million people, making it the sixth most populated state. Despite this large population, Illinois is nowhere near the largest state, being the 25th largest with 57,915 square miles under its jurisdiction.

Chicago, Illinois

Chicago is perhaps the most well-known part of Illinois, and is the most populated city within the state. The area in which Chicago resides is known as the Chicagoland area, and is home to 9.6 million people. Chicago is known for being diverse in population, and for being the third largest city in the United States.
